INTRODUCTION

Distillation columns are one of the most important components in processing units in refineries, gas processing complexes, and chemical plants. The production losses and unplanned shutdowns due to mechanical integrity and process related anomalies within the distillation columns result into huge financial losses. The sooner these problems identified enable the plant engineers to take correction measures to avoid production losses or unplanned shutdowns. Pre-shutdown scanning of these columns cut-down the turnaround timing by preparing the plant operators to procure the replacement of damage internals and arrange manpower to prevent prolonged delays.
Gamma scanning of a distillation column is a process diagnostic non-invasive and online inspection technique to investigate a column to check the process performance by determining the mechanical integrity of its internals and diagnosing any process related malfunctions or problems and enables plant engineers to optimize the production.

Main & Distinguishing Features of Gamma Scanning

  • It is an online line and non-invasive technique does not require any preparatory work.
  • Provides fast, cost effective, accurate, reliable and efficient results to pin-point problems within the distillation columns.
  • No need to remove the insulation or disrupt the normal column operations.
  • Accessibility to top of the column through external platforms and ladders in some rare cases require scaffolding.
  • Periodic gamma scanning of these columns provides continuous assessment monitoring of these columns.
  • Baseline scanning of the columns enables plant engineers to further troubleshoot the problems within the columns.
  • Prevents any unplanned shutdowns or loss of production.
  • Equipment used to investigate these columns is low voltage hand held intrinsically safe and battery operated tools do not require any special logistical requirements as well as easily and efficiently mobilized to sites.
  • Required radioactive isotopes are 1,000 -10,000 times smaller than the normally sources used for radiography and in compliance with local rules and regulations to govern working with radiation.
